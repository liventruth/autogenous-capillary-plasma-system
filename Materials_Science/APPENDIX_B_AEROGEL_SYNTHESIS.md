Appendix B: Materials Science & Core Synthesis

The system requires a highly porous chelation core to trap heavy metals. Synthetic polymers are too expensive. This protocol details the synthesis of a Chitosan-Cellulose Aerogel using only biological waste and ambient-pressure drying, achieving an ultra-low-cost core (under $0.50 per unit).

1. Raw Materials

Chitosan: Derived from chitin (crustacean shells, insect waste, or fungal/mushroom stalks).

Cellulose: Pulped plant fiber, bamboo, or recycled paper pulp.

Solvents: Dilute acetic acid (household vinegar) and ethanol (denatured or local high-proof spirits).

2. Synthesis Protocol (Low-Capital Method)

Step 1: Dissolution

Dissolve $2.0\%$ (wt/v) chitosan powder in a $1.0\%$ acetic acid aqueous solution. Stir mechanically or manually until a viscous, completely homogenous hydrogel forms.

Step 2: Cellulose Reinforcement

Blend in $10\%$ (wt/wt) cellulose pulp relative to the chitosan mass. This micro-fibrous reinforcement acts as a scaffold, preventing the aerogel from collapsing during the final drying phase.

Step 3: Gelation & Neutralization

Drip the chitosan-cellulose slurry into a mild $0.2\text{ M}$ Sodium Hydroxide ($\text{NaOH}$ / lye) bath. The rapid pH shift causes immediate cross-linking, forming solid hydrogel beads or molded pucks. Wash with clean water until the pH of the rinse water is neutral ($\text{pH} \approx 7$).

Step 4: Solvent Exchange (Crucial Step)

To avoid the massive expense of freeze-drying or supercritical $\text{CO}_2$ drying, we utilize ambient pressure drying (APD). Soak the hydrogels sequentially in water-ethanol mixtures:

$30\%$ ethanol (by volume) for 1 hour.

$50\%$ ethanol for 1 hour.

$70\%$ ethanol for 1 hour.

$100\%$ anhydrous ethanol for 1 hour.

This process replaces the water inside the molecular pores with ethanol.

Step 5: Ambient Drying

Because ethanol has a drastically lower surface tension than water, the alcohol-gels can be dried in a standard ambient oven at $60^\circ\text{C}$ or left in open air without capillary forces collapsing the microscopic pore structure.

3. Optical Resonance Calibration

By doping the chitosan matrix with $0.05\%$ dielectric micro-minerals (silica/titania), the core exhibits structural color. As heavy metals bind to the chitosan chains, the lattice swells, changing the refractive index of the material.

Optimal / Clean Core: Translucent white.

Saturation $\ge 85\%$: Iridescent dark crimson.

Action Required: Swap core and compost the spent biopolymer.
